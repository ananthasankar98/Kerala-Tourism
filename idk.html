Idukki

Idukki was formed on January 26, 1972 and is one of the 14 districts of the state of Kerala. The hilly district of Idukki is rich in beautiful hills and dense forests. Idukki is a storehouse of electricity for the people of Kerala. About 66 per cent of the state's energy needs are generated from various hydropower projects in Idukki. Idukki is the second largest district in the state of Kerala in the Western Ghats but lags far behind in terms of population density. Most of the areas of Idukki are forest land. Urban areas are densely populated but most of the villages are sparsely populated. Idukki is also the spice hub of Kerala. Though it is the second-largest district in the region in terms of area, it has the lowest population density among the districts of Kerala. The urban population is higher than the rural population. Idukki district lies amid the Cardamom Hills of Western Ghats in Kerala.
