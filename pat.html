Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta District, the thirteenth revenue district of the State of Kerala, nestles its head on the slopes of the Western Ghats and stretches to the low-lying rice fields bordering Alappuzha District. It is bounded on the North by Changanassery and Kanjirappally Taluks of Kottayam District and Peerumade Taluk of Idukki District, on the East by the State of Tamil Nadu, on the South by Kunnathur, Pathanapuram and Kottarakkara Taluks of Kollam District and on the West by Chengannur, Mavelikkara and Kuttanad Taluks of Alappuzha District. It abounds with natural splendours – undulating hills, dark mysterious forests, exotic wildlife and enchanting valleys. Pathanamthitta is a combination of two words – Pathanam and Thitta – which mean an array of houses on the river side. It is presumed that the regions presently under the District were formerly under the Pandalam reign which had connections with the Pandya Kingdom.

How to Reach


By road:There are regular buses from other major cities of the State to Pathanamthitta.


By rail: There is no regular trains to Pathanamthitta from other major cities of the country. The nearest Railway station is at Chengannur which is located at a distance of 20kms.


By air: Pathanamthitta is not well connected to other major cities of the country via regular flights. The nearest airport is in Trivandrum at a distance of 88kms.
