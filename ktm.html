Kottayam

Kottayam is a district in the southernmost state of India, Kerala. Situated in Central Kerala, this district is bordered by the majestic Western Ghats to the east, the Vembanad Lake to the west and the paddy fields of Kuttanad to its unique features. The area is associated with numerous legends, with sprawling backwaters, lush paddy fields, hills, ridges, hills, and extensive rubber plantations. Kottayam is also known as the Land of Letters, Likes and Latex in English. Kottayam is a major trading center for cash crops, especially rubber. Most of the natural rubber in India is produced from well-maintained plantations per acre in Kottayam district. The Rubber Board is also headquartered in Kottayam. Many of the first Malayalam daily newspapers, like Deepika, Malayala Manorama, and Mangalam, were started and are headquartered in Kottayam, as are a number of publishing houses.

How to Reach


By road: You can easily get regular buses to Kottayam from other major cities of the state.


By rail: Kottayam is well connected to major Indian cities by trains.


By air: Nearest airport is Kochi International Airport, Ernakulam district (90 km). Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, Thiruvananthapuram District (160 km)
